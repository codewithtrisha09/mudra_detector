About Bharatanatyam

Ancient roots

Bharatanatyam is one of India’s most celebrated classical dance forms, known for its grace, rhythm, and expressive storytelling.

Power of mudras

Mudras are the visual vocabulary of the dance, used to express objects, emotions, deities, and narrative meaning.

Expression and meaning

Each hand gesture carries symbolic significance, turning movement into a language that can communicate beyond words.

Why it matters

Understanding mudras preserves cultural heritage while making classical dance more accessible through modern technology.

Featured mudras: Pataka for a flag-like open hand, Anjali for salutation, Alapadma for a blooming lotus, Hamsasya for delicate expression, and Katakamukha for holding or pinching gestures.
